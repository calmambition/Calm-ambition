The work I do

In 2019, I was working full-time across some of the most demanding professional environments in the world while completing my MBA at Rotman. I was performing at the highest level, and quietly falling apart. I managed it the way most high performers do: I pushed harder, slept less, told myself it was temporary.

What I didn't understand then, and what I now help my clients understand, is that what I was experiencing was not a weakness to overcome. It was a system running without the fuel it needed. That experience didn't just teach me about burnout. It changed everything about how I understand high performance.

Today I work with people who are very good at what they do, who push hard, and who are starting to wonder how much longer they can keep this up. Together we build the internal infrastructure that makes excellence sustainable through the Calm Ambition Method: Surface, Stabilise, Sustain.

I am not a therapist. I am not a wellness brand. I am someone who has sat in the same rooms as you, and found a way through.

The Calm Ambition Method

Three phases.
One transformation.

Most burnout advice tells you to rest more and stress less. That is not a strategy. The Calm Ambition Method gives you a structured path from chronic strain to a way of working that actually holds across 12 weeks of private coaching.

01
Phase One
Surface
Weeks 1–4

You cannot work with what you cannot name. The Surface phase brings into precise, named awareness the patterns that have been running below the level of conscious attention: the stress triggers, the energy leaks, the habits keeping you stuck in overperformance.

Most high performers arrive knowing something is off. Very few have been able to articulate exactly what. That changes here.

What this phase includes
  • Burnout dimension mapping across exhaustion, detachment, and efficacy
  • Personal stress trigger identification
  • Body-based early-warning signal awareness
  • Stress timeline: when this actually started
02
Phase Two
Stabilise
Weeks 5–8

Insight without regulation is not enough. A person who understands their pattern but remains depleted will struggle to change anything because behaviour change under chronic depletion is genuinely difficult. Stabilise is about building the capacity to return to a calm baseline faster, more reliably, and without requiring a crisis to catalyse it.

Regulation comes before strategy. This is where consistent action starts to become possible again.

What this phase includes
  • Evidence-based physiological regulation tools
  • Recovery architecture: what genuine recovery actually looks like for you
  • Identity work around rest and permission to recover
  • One tool, one week: building through practice not theory
03
Phase Three
Sustain
Weeks 9–12

The gap between insight and durable change is where most coaching efforts collapse. Sustain is about closing that gap by building durable behaviour change embedded in the architecture of your professional life.

This is not about motivation. It is about making the new pattern easy enough that the old one stops being the default. The work ends with a 90-day sustainability roadmap, a document you keep, not a promise you make.

What this phase includes
  • Values-calendar audit: where your energy is actually going
  • Environmental architecture: removing friction from your recovery practices
  • Relapse signal identification and pre-decided response protocol
  • 90-Day Sustainability Roadmap: your final session deliverable

About Coaching
What is the difference between coaching and therapy?+
Coaching is not therapy and does not treat clinical conditions. Coaching works with functional people on future-focused goals: building capacity, changing patterns, and improving performance. If you are experiencing significant mental health symptoms or are in crisis, clinical support is the appropriate first step, and I will always say that directly if I believe it applies.
What does a coaching session actually look like?+
Sessions are 60 minutes, held via video call. They are structured around what is live for you right now, informed by the current phase of the method. I will ask direct questions, reflect back what I hear with precision, and give you clear, specific practices to work with between sessions. These are not conversations that stay comfortable if comfort is not serving you.
Why work with someone who has a corporate background?+
Because the people I work with are not struggling in a vacuum. They are struggling inside specific environments with specific demands. Fifteen years across KPMG, Deloitte, Edelman, and CIBC means I understand those environments from the inside. I know what the pressure looks like, what the culture asks of you, and what the standard generic wellness advice misses entirely. That context matters.
The Programme
How long is the programme and what does it include?+
The Calm Ambition 3-Month Programme runs for 12 weeks. It includes bi-weekly 60-minute private coaching sessions, working through all three phases of the Calm Ambition Method. Between sessions you have access to voice note and email support, and you receive custom tools, practices, and reflection prompts appropriate to your current phase. The final session produces a 90-day sustainability roadmap.
What are the three phases?+
Surface (Weeks 1–4): naming what has been running below awareness. Stabilise (Weeks 5–8): building the regulatory capacity to interrupt chronic stress cycles and begin genuine recovery. Sustain (Weeks 9–12): embedding what works into the architecture of your professional life so the change holds after the engagement ends.
What is the investment?+
Pricing for the three-month programme is shared during the discovery call. It is not listed on the website. For the calibre of professional this work is designed for, a conversation before pricing is both appropriate and useful. Book a discovery call to discuss.
Logistics
Where are sessions held?+
All sessions are held remotely via video call. I am based in Sydney, Australia and work with clients globally. Sessions are scheduled to accommodate different time zones.
How many clients do you work with at a time?+
I work with a small number of clients concurrently to ensure the quality of attention each engagement requires. If you are considering it, having the conversation sooner is worth it.
Is This Right For Me?
I'm still functioning well at work. Is this for me?+
Yes. The people who get the most from this work are those who are still performing, still delivering, still showing up, but who can feel the current pattern is not sustainable. Waiting until you have hit the wall means the intervention has to be larger and the recovery longer. If you can feel the drift, this is the right time.
What if I've already tried therapy, books, or other approaches?+
That is often what brings people here. Most high performers have tried the standard approaches: therapy for a period, self-help frameworks, the holiday that didn't reset anything. What is different here is that this work is designed specifically for people like you, inside environments like yours, with a structured methodology built around how high-performing nervous systems actually work.
How do I know if this is the right fit?+
That is what the discovery call is for. It is a 30-minute conversation, no pitch, no pressure. I will ask you directly what is going on, listen carefully, and give you my honest read on whether this engagement makes sense. If it is not the right fit, I will say so and point you somewhere more appropriate.
