The work I do

In 2019, I was working full-time across some of the most demanding professional environments in the world while completing my MBA at Rotman. I was performing at the highest level, and quietly falling apart. I managed it the way most high performers do: I pushed harder, slept less, told myself it was temporary.

What I didn't understand then, and what I now help my clients understand, is that what I was experiencing was not a weakness to overcome. It was a system running without the fuel it needed. Today I work with people who are very good at what they do, who push hard, and who are starting to wonder how much longer they can keep this up. Together we build the internal infrastructure that makes excellence sustainable through the Calm Ambition Method: Surface, Stabilise, Sustain.

The Calm Ambition Method

Three phases.
One transformation.

Most burnout advice tells you to rest more and stress less. That is not a strategy. The Calm Ambition Method gives you a structured path from chronic strain to a way of working that actually holds across 12 weeks of private coaching.

01
Phase One
Surface
Weeks 1-4

You cannot work with what you cannot name. The Surface phase brings into precise, named awareness the patterns that have been running below the level of conscious attention: the stress triggers, the energy leaks, the habits keeping you stuck in overperformance.

Most high performers arrive knowing something is off. Very few have been able to articulate exactly what. That changes here.

What this phase includes
  • Burnout dimension mapping across exhaustion, detachment, and efficacy
  • Personal stress trigger identification
  • Body-based early-warning signal awareness
  • Stress timeline: when this actually started
02
Phase Two
Stabilise
Weeks 5-8

Insight without regulation is not enough. A person who understands their pattern but remains depleted will struggle to change anything because behaviour change under chronic depletion is genuinely difficult. Stabilise is about building the capacity to return to a calm baseline faster, more reliably, and without requiring a crisis to catalyse it.

Regulation comes before strategy. This is where consistent action starts to become possible again.

What this phase includes
  • Evidence-based physiological regulation tools
  • Recovery architecture: what genuine recovery actually looks like for you
  • Identity work around rest and permission to recover
  • One tool, one week: building through practice not theory
03
Phase Three
Sustain
Weeks 9-12

The gap between insight and durable change is where most coaching efforts collapse. Sustain is about closing that gap by building durable behaviour change embedded in environmental architecture.

This is not about motivation. It is about making the new pattern easy enough that the old one stops being the default. The work ends with a 90-day sustainability roadmap, a document you keep, not a promise you make.

What this phase includes
  • Values-calendar audit: where your energy is actually going
  • Environmental architecture: removing friction from your recovery practices
  • Relapse signal identification and pre-decided response protocol
  • 90-Day Sustainability Roadmap: your final session deliverable
